Astrophysics | Analysis of X-Ray Binary Systems

Active Study 01/2026

Stars are rarely born alone, often existing in pairs. When one of these stars reaches the end of its life, it can collapse into a compact object (e.g., black holes (BH) or neutron stars). This creates a Black Hole X-ray Binary (BHXRB), a system where the BH feeds off the mass of the donor star. As material starts spinning around the BH its gets hotter and faster, creating an accretion disk which emits X-rays, while some of this material gets launched into powerful jets visible in the radio wavelength regime. How these jets are exactly formed is not yet known.

This project investigates specific BHXRBs with data from the Atacama Large Millimeter Array (ALMA) radio telescope. I wrote a script that automates the data retrieval and processing of calibrated datasets to plot target visibilities to identify emission lines. By using imaging techniques to map these emission lines from the environment around the BH, where the jets slam into the interstellar medium (ISM), we are able to deconstruct how jets are formed and how powerful they are.

Computer Vision | Image + Video Processing of Lab Mice

Completed 01/2026 - 05/2026

Developed a Python-based computer vision pipeline for image processing using YOLO and OpenCV to automate behavioral tracking and spatial analysis of lab mice in real-time video. Engineered custom polygon zone mapping and ID logic across 1,000+ frames, optimizing performance via Metal Performance Shaders (MPS) acceleration. Streamlined high- precision dataset development by building an interactive automation tool for custom video selection and frame-by-frame progression. Programmed the system to simultaneously export standardized ground-truth labels in COCO JSON, YOLO (.txt), and Pascal VOC (.xml) formats, ensuring readiness and data integrity for interdisciplinary neuroscience research.

Theoretical Physics | Cosmology

Completed 05/2023 - 06/2025

Participated with the Theoretical Physics Group seminars on quantum mechanics and cosmology. I conducted directed independent studies on stellar evolution and collapse mechanisms and black hole dynamics, followed by an in-depth analysis and literature reviews of the geometric formalism of Special Relativity (Minkowski metrics) and the classic experimental verifications of General Relativity. Later expanding to a foundational study of quantum gravity candidates. I also assisted with analysis with how the Generalized Uncertainty Principle induces observable dips in ultrahigh energy neutrino spectra and reviewing for evidence of sterile neutrino signatures and analyzing how matter-enhanced oscillations (MSW effect) influence neutrino survival probabilities in dense environments.

Quantum Computing | Simulating Circuits

Completed 05/2023 - 06/2024

Utilizing the standard Nielsen and Chuang text, I conducted a rigorous study of quantum information fundamentals, manually deriving matrix representations for single and multi-qubit gates. I successfully implemented and simulated key algorithms including the Deutsch-Jozsa, Shor’s, and HHL using Python’s Qiskit library and IBM’s cloud quantum processors. My work concluded in a comprehensive set of textbook solutions and functional circuits demonstrating quantum teleportation and arithmetic operations.
